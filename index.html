About me

I study Mathematics at the University of Oxford and tutor students who want to become more confident, precise and effective at maths. My approach is simple: first make the idea feel intuitive, then practise enough exam-style questions that the method becomes automatic.

I work especially well with students who are aiming for top GCSE and A-Level grades, or who want support with more advanced problem-solving for university admissions tests.

How lessons work

Lessons are online, one-to-one and tailored to the student. The aim is not just to get through questions, but to understand exactly where mistakes are coming from and fix them.

Diagnose

We start by identifying the student’s current level, target grade and weakest topics.

Explain

I explain the key ideas clearly, using examples that build from simple to exam-level.

Practise

We work through targeted questions, focusing on method, presentation and exam technique.

Frequently asked questions

Lessons are online, usually using video call and a shared whiteboard. This makes scheduling easier and allows students to learn from home.

I can support students studying AQA, Edexcel, OCR and other major UK exam boards. Lessons are adapted to the student’s syllabus.

Do you offer a trial lesson?

I am happy to discuss the student’s goals first by email and arrange an initial session to see whether the tutoring is a good fit.

Can you help with Oxbridge maths preparation?

Yes. I can help with MAT-style problem solving, TMUA preparation, STEP questions and interview-style mathematical discussion.
